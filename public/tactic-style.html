TACTIC STYLE
    CONTROL POSSESSION
  • aims to dictate play
  • patiently wait for an opening
  • focuses on retaining possession and pressing high to win the ball back
  • looks to play out of the defense to create chances
  • short-passing build up play with 4-2-3-1 wide, and 5-2-2-1 wing back
    •
    GENGEN PRESS
  • aims to win back possession
  • High Intensity Running
  • Relies on every member of the team working hard
  • Being fit and mobile enough to press he opposition immediately after losing the ball
  • short-passing build up play with 4-2-3-1 wide, and 5-2-2-1 wing back
    •
    TIKI-TAKA
  • Dominate possession
  • Relentless pressing
  • Patiently wait for openings
  • Emphasis on short passing
  • Extreme pressing and movement
  • Waiting for space to open as opponent loses focus
  • an extreme variant of the control possession style with 4-1-4-1 dm wide, 4-2-3-1 wide and 5-2-2-1 wingback formations
    • VERTICAL TIKI-TAKA
  • Dominate possession
  • Relentless pressing
  • Patiently wait for openings
  • emphasis on moving the ball vertically rather than side-to-side
  • quick passing playing style of possession and movementt
  • Waiting for space to open as opponent loses focus
  • a narrower more direct approach than the standard tiki-taka with 4-1-4-1 dm wide, 4-4-2 diamond narrow and 5-2-1-2 wing back formations
    • WING PLAY
  • looks to get quality crosses into the box
  • overloads the wide areas
  • Patiently wait for openings
  • players will look to get the ball out wide as early as possible full backs will look to overlap and exploit the wide areas 4-4-2, 4-1-4-1 dm wide and 4-2-4 wide formations
    • ROUTE ONE
  • quick transition
  • direct passing
  • aims to play the furthest forward pass available
  • intent to penetrate the opponent as quick as possible
    • FLUID COUNTER-ATTACK
  • soak up pressure
  • more expressive and fluid counter attacks
  • looks to draw opposition forward to leave them vulnerable on the break
  • players will be fluid on the counter
  • trying to combine with each other and carry the ball with clear intent 4-1-4-1 dm wide, 4-4-1-1 and 5-3-2 wingback formations
    • DIRECT COUNTER-ATTACK
  • soak up pressure
  • direct and structured counter-attacks
  • draws the opposition forward to leave them vulnerable on the break
  • players will look to move the ball forward as quickly as possible on the counter attack
  • exploit spaces in behind 4-4-2, 4-1-4-1 dm wide and 4-4-1-1 formations
    • CANTENACCIO
  • looks to nullify the oppositions attacking threat
  • highly-structured defensive approach
  • draws the opposition forward to leave them vulnerable on the break
  • a defense-focused style that primarily looks t deny the opposition goalscoring opportunities
  • a libero is traditionally deployed to offer cover behind the defensive line 5-3-2 wing back, 5-1-2-2dm wide wing back and 5-4-1 wingback wide
    • PARK THE BUS
  • low and structured defensive shape
  • restrict space in the final third
  • draws the opposition forward to leave them vulnerable on the break
  • players will look to get behind the ball and protect their defensive third
  • focuses more on clean sheets than on possession 4-4-2, 4-1-4-1, and 4-2-3-1 dm formations
